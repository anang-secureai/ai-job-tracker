What we count

Included

First-time layoffs where AI was a material factor — either explicitly cited by the company, or identified by credible reporting as a primary driver.

Excluded

Restated totals, duplicate announcements, and layoffs attributed solely to non-AI factors such as macroeconomic conditions or organizational restructuring.

How we classify AI involvement

Explicit

The company publicly cited AI in an official statement, SEC filing, or on-the-record comment.

Blamed

A credible outlet (AP, Reuters, major business press) identified AI as a primary driver — even if the company didn't say so directly.

Mixed

AI is cited alongside other factors — restructuring, market conditions — as a partial but not sole driver.

For Allies

Ally Toolkit

Talking points, messaging guidance, and platform-ready copy for amplifying the tracker.

Talking Points

  • Lead with the data, not the organization
  • Make it personal without fearmongering — "is yours on the list?" not "you're going to lose your job"
  • Acknowledge the methodology is transparent — invite skeptics to check it

Do

  • Link to jobloss.ai
  • Cite specific numbers from the dashboard
  • Use the name "AI Job Loss Tracker"
  • Mention it's free and open
  • Tag @secureainow
  • Use your own voice and style

Don't

  • Call it "anti-AI"
  • Imply there's a paywall
  • Claim the data as yours
  • Round up statistics
  • Dismiss methodology questions
  • Post once and forget — share updates
